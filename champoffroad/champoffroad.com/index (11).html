World Championship Off-Road Races Event History & Preview

haley August 30, 2021 Event Preview Leave a Comment

The 2021 AMSOIL Championship Off-Road season has reached its finale weekend, and what better place for a finale than the Crandon International Off-Road Raceway on Labor Day weekend. This will be the 5th time in the last seven years that Crandon has hosted a points season finale on Labor Day weekend. 

Crandon International’s World Championship weekend is the biggest event in short course off-road racing, bringing hundreds of race teams and tens of thousands of fans to the small town of fewer than 2,000 people. This weekend’s 52nd annual edition of the event is set to be one of the biggest yet. 

As is always the case, Fall Crandon brings out more competitors than any other event. Past short course champions like Bryce Menzies and Brian Deegan always make time for the event, as do stars from other racing disciplines. Stay tuned for the complete entry list this Wednesday 9/1.  

Just one season championship has already been decided ahead of this weekend, meaning 15 other points championship battles will come to a close at the Big House on Saturday. In addition to Champ Off-Road’s 16 classes, fans will be treated to 3 ULTRA4 classes and the EMPI Class 11 Volkswagens this weekend. 

CJ Greaves swept at Bark River to extend his PRO4 points lead to 25 points over Kyle LeDuc. Greaves can clinch his 6th career PRO4 title with a finish of 11th or better in Round 11. Crandon has been a stomping ground for Greaves, as he is a multi-time World Champion in 5 different classes. 

Bryce Menzies and Keegan Kincaid will rejoin the PRO4 field this weekend, adding to an already stacked lineup. Menzies is a three-time PRO2 World Champion, a two-time PRO4 World Champion, and was the 2017 Crandon Cup winner, while Kincaid picked up a PRO4 win at Crandon back in 2015. CJ Greaves holds three titles, and Johnny, LeDuc, and RJ Anderson each with two.

Jerett Brooks extended his PRO2 points lead at Bark River, enough that a finish of 8th or better in the final round will secure his 3rd PRO2 championship. Brooks is always a frontrunner at Crandon, with wins in the 2019 PRO2 World Championship, Round 7 in 2020, and the Potawatomi Cup earlier this season. 

Six different drivers have won PRO2 races in the 2021 season, the highest total in years. Only Keegan Kincaid and Ryan Beat have graced the top of the podium multiple times this season, but multiple DNFs for each have them needing miracles to win the championship. 

Brock Heger put together yet another solid weekend in PRO-LITE at Bark River, this time clinching his 2nd career PRO-LITE championship one round early in the process. Heger’s Round 10 win was his seventh of the season. 

The last 7 seasons of PRO-LITE competition have seen 7 different drivers crowned World Champion on Labor Day Weekend at Crandon. Brock Heger, Christopher Polvoorde, and Zack Szymik will try to break that streak, while the rest of the field charges to continue it. 


Andrew Carlson experienced a nightmare coming true in Round 9 at Bark River when he sustained his first Pro Mod SxS DNF of the season. Despite avenging it with a Round 10 win, he saw his points lead slip away and he now sits 3rd in the standings behind the father/son VanEperen team.

Mechanical issues for CJ Greaves in Round 9 coupled with another weekend sweep for Brock Heger has all but sealed the Pro Stock SxS championship for Heger. A finish of 22nd or better will secure the title and make Heger a double champion in his first season racing in the midwest. 

Despite winning only two races this season, former champion Rodney VanEperen finds himself atop the Pro Mod SxS points standings with just one round of racing to go. With a finish of 8th or better in Round 11, VanEperen will claim his second Pro Mod SxS championship. 

Picking up steam late in the season in Pro Stock SxS is Jacob Rosales. A solid weekend that included a podium at Bark River has launched the Yamaha driver into 4th in points, just 13 markers back of 3rd place Owen VanEperen. Rosales finished 3rd at Crandon earlier this season in Round 3. 

Among the most shocking storylines of the 2021 season has been the misfortune of former Pro Mod SxS champion Kyle Chaney. Chaney once again suffered a mechanical failure while leading at Bark River, leaving him 13th in points. With the points championship out of reach, Chaney will focus on winning his first-ever Crandon World Championship. 

Owen VanEperen’s strong Bark River weekend in Pro Stock, which featured a 2nd place finish, has propelled him into 3rd in points in just his second season in the highly competitive class. VanEperen has a chance to finish Top 3 in points in both Pro SxS classes this weekend, and will also hope to defend his 2020 Pro Stock World Championship.
